Objective

The objective of Lab 4 was to create a light sensitive robot that could detect and follow a line by differentiating between white and black surfaces. The lab challenged us to utilize previous skills learned over the quarter to build and assemble hardware, and design and program software for our robot.

Challenge 3: Designing the Software

Challenge 3 focused on designing our software. To send information to and from our robot, we calibrated the sensors and programmed the PID control. This allowed our robot to detect and follow a line. And to compensate for any possible errors, this allowed our robot to adjust its turn rate.

Why do you need to calibrate your photoresistor values?
Calibration is essential. In order for our robot to detect and follow a line it needed to differentiate between white and black surfaces. The calibration code enabled our robot to do so.

What problem does this help solve?
Calibration created uniform values, which helped our robot compare oncoming error values to, adjusting the turn rates appropriately.

What problems do you still see coming from your photoresistors?
There is a possible problem that the photoresistor readings may have disproportionate values amongst each other despite being calibrated. This can be due to a poor distribution of internal LED exposure.


What does PID stand for?
Proportion
Integral
Derivative

What is the purpose of P, I, and D?
Proportion control took the current error values and scaled it by our proportional gain. This transformed our errors into a desired turn rate.

Integral control took the sum of the past errors, acting as the "memory" for the errors.

Derivative control predicted an increase or decrease in error values and prepared the turn rate in advance.

By recognizing these improving or worsening values, PID control increased or decreased the amount of additional right or left motor speed accordingly. Thus, altering the turn rate to create a more efficent anf effective line detection response.

What steps did you take to choose the best value for your robot?
Instead of hard coding values, we chose the best values for our robot based on trial and error.
